The Genealogy of Jesus Christ

The direct line from Adam to Jesus, 77 generations across 4,000+ years of scripture.

Scripture Sources

This genealogy is compiled from the following key passages (MEV):

  • Genesis 5 -- Adam to Noah (Pre-Flood patriarchs)
  • Genesis 11 -- Shem to Abram (Post-Flood patriarchs)
  • Ruth 4:18-22 -- Perez to David
  • Matthew 1 -- Abraham to Jesus through Joseph (royal/legal line)
  • Luke 3:23-38 -- Jesus to Adam through Mary (biological line)

The Line Divides After David

After King David, scripture records two genealogical lines to Jesus. Matthew’s Gospel traces the royal/legal line through Solomon to Joseph, making Jesus the legal heir to David's throne. Luke’s Gospel traces the biological line through Nathan to Mary, making Jesus a physical descendant of David.

Jesus Christ

"The Son of David, the Son of Abraham"

Born in Bethlehem of Judea. Legal heir to David's throne through Joseph. Biological descendant of David through Mary. The promised Messiah.

Matthew 1:1 • Luke 3:23-38

Why Two Different Genealogies?

Matthew and Luke record different genealogies for Jesus. The widely held explanation is that Matthew traces the legal/royal line through Joseph, making Jesus the legal heir to David's throne, while Luke traces the biological line through Mary, making Jesus a physical descendant of David. In Jewish culture, a genealogy could be listed under the husband's name even when tracing the wife's lineage. Luke 3:23 carefully notes that Jesus was "the son, so it was thought, of Joseph."

Both lines converge at David and earlier at Abraham, confirming that Jesus fulfilled the messianic prophecies requiring the Messiah to be both a son of Abraham (Genesis 22:18) and a son of David (2 Samuel 7:12-16).

The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the Son of David, the Son of Abraham. -- Matthew 1:1 (MEV)
